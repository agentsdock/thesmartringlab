## Accuracy expectations

**All smart rings have similar accuracy limitations.**

What they do well:
- Sleep trend tracking (when you slept, rough sleep stages)
- Resting heart rate and HRV trends
- Activity and step counts (rough estimates)
- Temperature trends (nighttime)

What they don't do well:
- Workout heart rate accuracy (optical sensors struggle during movement)
- Medical-grade sleep staging (it's pattern recognition, not clinical EEG)
- Real-time stress measurement (HRV is a proxy, not direct)

If workout HR accuracy matters, get a chest strap (Polar H10, Garmin HRM-Pro). No ring competes with a chest strap for exercise.

## Who gets disappointed by smart rings

**Common disappointment patterns:**

1. **"I expected perfect sleep staging"**
   Smart rings estimate sleep stages based on movement and HRV. They're not EEG. If you need clinical sleep data, this isn't the device.

2. **"My workout heart rate is wrong"**
   Optical sensors struggle during movement. This is true for all rings, all watches. Chest straps are the solution.

3. **"The app keeps asking me to pay"**
   Oura's subscription model frustrates users who expected "buy once, use forever." RingConn avoids this.

4. **"Sizing was wrong and now I can't return"**
   Most brands offer sizing kits. Use them. Don't guess your ring size based on jewelry rings.

5. **"It doesn't sync with my other apps"**
   Check compatibility before buying. RingConn syncs with Apple Health and Google Fit. Oura has broader integration. Samsung Galaxy Ring is limited to Samsung Health.

## FAQ

**Q: Do smart rings work without a phone?**
A: They track data without a phone, but you need a phone to sync and see the data. They don't work standalone like a smartwatch.

**Q: Can I wear a smart ring on any finger?**
A: Most brands recommend index or middle finger. Ring finger has too much movement. Thumb has unusual shape for sensors.

**Q: Are smart rings waterproof?**
A: Most are water-resistant for showers and swimming. RingConn is rated for swimming. Oura is water-resistant but not recommended for prolonged water exposure.

**Q: Do smart rings track steps accurately?**
A: Roughly, but not as accurate as a phone or watch. Rings count steps based on hand movement, which can be misleading if your hands are stationary while walking.

**Q: Which ring has the best app?**
A: Oura's app is the most polished. RingConn's app is functional but less refined. Samsung's app is good if you use Samsung Health.
