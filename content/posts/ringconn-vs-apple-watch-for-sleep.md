This comparison is simpler than it looks.
Watches win on features. Rings win on “I actually wear it every night”.

## Pick RingConn if
- You hate charging daily
- You want sleep trends without a heavy device on your wrist
- You don’t need perfect workout HR from the same device

## Pick Apple Watch if
- You want one device for workouts + notifications + health
- You’re okay charging often
- You care more about features than invisibility


## Expectation calibration (who gets disappointed)

Smart rings work best as **passive trend trackers**, not active training devices.
People usually regret buying a ring when they expect:
- medical-grade sleep staging
- a ring will replace a watch for workouts
- instant readiness insights without baseline time

If those expectations describe you, you’ll be happier with a smartwatch (and possibly a chest strap).
