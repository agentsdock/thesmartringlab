People search “is RingConn waterproof” because they don’t want to baby the device.
The practical question is: what do you *actually* do daily, and what is the risk if you stop thinking about it?

## The real-world rule
If you’re unsure, treat water exposure as “usually fine”, but avoid the worst combinations: heat + chemicals + time.

## Shower
Usually low risk, but soap buildup and slipping are the annoying parts.
Many people take rings off in the shower simply because it feels slippery.

## Swimming
It’s less about water and more about losing the ring. If your size is even slightly loose, swimming is where regret happens.

## Sauna / hot tubs
Heat is the risk multiplier. Even if a device is water resistant, high heat cycles are not friendly long-term.

## Tiny honesty
Some users stop wearing any ring around water, not because the device will fail, but because they hate the “what if” anxiety.
