People search sleep accuracy because they see one weird night and lose trust.
The healthier approach is to treat sleep data as a trend tool, then reduce the common noise sources.

## What’s usually useful
- Total sleep time trend over weeks
- Bedtime consistency patterns
- Recovery direction paired with HR/HRV trends

## What’s usually misleading
- Treating one night’s deep/REM split as “truth”
- Assuming every wake-up should be detected perfectly


### Mini: how sizing affects data accuracy

Smart rings use optical sensors. Those sensors need **stable skin contact**.
When the ring is slightly loose:
- small gaps let ambient light leak in
- the ring rotates, so the sensor reads different spots
- movement changes pressure, which changes the signal
- sleep makes it worse because you roll and flex for hours

Fit is not just comfort, fit is signal quality.


## Tiny honesty
If you read your sleep charts every morning and feel worse, you may be better off checking weekly.
